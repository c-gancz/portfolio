DESIGN PORTFOLIO

Spectrum Design Exhibition

2016 // ENVIRONMENTAL GRAPHICS

The mission behind this project was to produce a design exhibition featuring work from graduating students in the Design and Computation Arts program at Concordia. This project was in collaboration with other students in an exhibition design class, and I chose to take part in the environmental graphics team. Our task involved producing wayfinding and graphic design in the space that would allow the exhibition viewers to understand and engage with various elements in the space. The theme of the exhibition was “Spectrum”, as the Design and Computation Arts program at Concordia allows for students to learn a wide spectrum of design disciplines, such as graphic, industrial, soft-surface, and interaction design. The result of this multidisciplinary approach always results in the students producing multifaceted work that is quite unique and appealing.

Spectrum Design

Exhibition

2016 // ENVIRONMENTAL GRAPHICS

SUMMARY

BRIEF

CONTRIBUTIONS

The mission behind this project was to produce a design exhibition featuring work from graduating students in the Design and Computation Arts program at Concordia. This project was in collaboration with other students in an exhibition design class, and I chose to take part in the environmental graphics team. Our task involved producing wayfinding and graphic design in the space that would allow the exhibition viewers to understand and engage with various elements in the space. The theme of the exhibition was “Spectrum”, as the Design and Computation Arts program at Concordia allows for students to learn a wide spectrum of design disciplines, such as graphic, industrial, soft-surface, and interaction design. The result of this multidisciplinary approach always results in the students producing multifaceted work that is quite unique and appealing.

Gradients became a main graphic design element that was used along all elements of the exhibition branding, as a gradient of colours demonstrated a wide spectrum of possibilities. We used this element in our process as we sketched and tried to find different ways of expressing this in our wayfinding elements. We also wanted to promote the wide range of disciplines available in the Design program by researching materials we could use that would elaborate this theme in the exhibition. We developed ideas that incorporated three dimensional design with two dimensional design, keeping in mind all the different meanings the word “Spectrum” could represent. Our final design for the wayfinding became three dimensional wooden structures that were held erect by cement and screen-printed with the exhibition logo.

Modifying the existing exhibition logo to look appealing on the wooden panel in the structure, as well as adjusting the colours for the screen-printing process Manually screen-printing the wooden boards Designing the elevator wayfinding elements, including preparing the files for die-cutting and installing them in the elevators Designing the work descriptions, taking into account existing branding elements of the exhibition and important graphic design values
